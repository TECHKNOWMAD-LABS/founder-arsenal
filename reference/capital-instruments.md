# Capital Instruments Deep Reference

## SAFE Notes (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)

### Market Position (2025)
- 90% of pre-seed deals on Carta
- 64% of seed rounds
- 85% post-money SAFEs (vs pre-money)
- 61% use valuation cap only (no discount)

### Post-Money SAFE Mechanics
```
Investor Ownership = Investment Amount / Post-Money Valuation Cap

Example:
- $500K investment on $10M post-money cap
- Investor owns exactly 5% ($500K / $10M)
- This holds regardless of additional SAFEs raised
```

### Key Advantages
- No interest, no maturity date
- Rolling close capability (accept money as it comes)
- No lawyer typically needed
- Fast execution (days, not weeks)
- Post-money version provides dilution certainty

### Key Risks
- No board seat or governance rights
- No maturity date means no pressure to convert (could sit forever)
- Stacking multiple SAFEs compounds dilution at conversion
- Pro-rata side letters add complexity

### Standard Terms (YC SAFE, 2025)
- YC deal: $125K on post-money SAFE for 7% + $375K uncapped SAFE with MFN
- Median cap: $10M for $1M raised
- No discount is most common (61% of SAFEs)
- When discount used: typically 15-20%

## Convertible Notes

### When to Use (vs SAFE)
- Bridge rounds between priced rounds
- When investors require debt protections
- When interest deduction benefits the startup
- Institutional investors sometimes prefer notes

### Standard Terms
- Interest rate: 2-8% annually
- Maturity: 12-24 months
- Discount: 15-25% to next round
- Valuation cap: negotiated (comparable to SAFE caps)

### Key Difference from SAFE
```
$300K convertible note at 5% interest, 24-month maturity:
- At conversion: $300K + $30K interest = $330K converts to equity
- vs SAFE: $300K converts (no interest accrual)
- Net cost to founder: $30K additional dilution over 2 years
```

### Convertible Note Issuance Trend
- Surged to $48B in 2024
- Declining as % of early-stage (SAFEs dominating)
- Still preferred for bridge rounds and institutional bridges

## Priced Equity Rounds

### When to Use
- Series A and beyond (institutional expectation)
- Raising $5M+ from named funds
- Need to convert stacked SAFEs/notes (clean up cap table)
- Company has enough data to support valuation negotiation

### Standard Structure
- Preferred stock with liquidation preference
- Board seat(s) for lead investor
- Protective provisions (veto rights)
- Anti-dilution protections
- Information rights
- Pro-rata rights

### Process Timeline
- 2-6 months from start to close
- Legal costs: $25-75K (startup side), $15-50K (investor side, often charged to startup)

## Venture Debt

### Market Overview (2025-2026)
- Total rates: SOFR + 6-9% (8-15% annually)
- SOFR base rate: ~4.5%
- Loan terms: 2-3 year facilities
- Interest-only period: 6-12 months
- Warrant coverage: 0.1-0.5% of company

### Major Lenders
| Lender | Focus | Min Revenue |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Hercules Capital | Tech + life sciences growth | $5M+ ARR |
| JPMorgan (post-SVB) | Broad tech | $750K min deposits |
| TriplePoint | Venture lending | Varies |
| Arc Capital Markets | Marketplace (100+ lenders) | Varies |

### When to Use
- Post-equity round, need to extend runway 6-12 months
- Finance specific growth initiatives (expansion, equipment)
- Preserve 60-80% more founder equity vs equity round
- Bridge to profitability or next milestone

### Key Risks
- Covenants can restrict operations
- Repayment obligation regardless of company performance
- Default triggers can force fire-sale
- Warrants still create some dilution

## Revenue-Based Financing (RBF)

### Provider Comparison

| Provider | Model | Fee | Requirements | Best For |
|----------|-------|-----|-------------|----------|
| Capchase | Direct lender | 5-12% of advance | $1M+ ARR | SaaS |
| Pipe | Marketplace | Variable | Recurring revenue | SaaS, subscriptions |
| Clearco | Revenue share | 5-8% | Revenue history | E-commerce, DTC |

### Mechanics
```
Example (Capchase):
- $1M ARR SaaS company
- Advance: Up to $700K (70% of ARR)
- Fee: 8% ($56K)
- Repayment: Percentage of monthly revenue until $756K repaid
- Growing revenue = faster repayment
- Slow month = smaller payment
```

### Advantages
- Zero dilution
- No board seats
- No personal guarantees (usually)
- Fast disbursement (days)
- Scales with revenue

## Token Instruments (Crypto/Web3)

### Instrument Comparison
| Instrument | Structure | Regulation | Best For |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| SAFT | Future token delivery | SEC private placement | Pre-launch tokens |
| Token Warrant | Right to buy tokens | Flexible | Post-launch or pre-launch |
| ICO | Direct token sale | Varies (declining) | Community distribution |
| STO | Security token | SEC regulated | Asset-backed tokens |
| IDO | DEX listing | Minimal | Community-first projects |

### SAFT vs Token Warrant
- SAFT: Tokens auto-deliver when blockchain goes live
- Token Warrant: Investor chooses when to exercise (tax optimization)
- Hybrid SAFE+T: Traditional equity SAFE + token warrant combined

### 2025-2026 Crypto Fundraising
- $25B+ raised in crypto in 2025
- Hybrid structures increasingly common
- Projects distribute tokens before open-market trading
- Regulatory clarity improving but jurisdiction-dependent

### Reg CF Limits (2025)
- Max raise: $5M per 12-month period
- Available to non-accredited investors
- Required: SEC Form C filing
- Ongoing reporting requirements

### Strategic Use
- Community building (investors become customers)
- Social proof for institutional rounds
- GTM channel, not just capital
- Can combine with institutional round (hybrid)

## SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle)

### Mechanics
- Single-purpose entity pools capital from multiple investors
- Invest in one company, one round
- Lead earns carried interest (up to 15%)
- Setup: ~$8K + $2K regulatory fee

### Platforms
- AngelList Venture (incumbent)
- Sydecar (emerging)
- Allocations (fast/flexible)

### Use Cases
- Aggregate angel checks ($5K-$25K) to meet startup minimums ($100K+)
- Sidecar SPVs alongside institutional funds
- Access deals through syndicate leads

## Rolling Funds

### Mechanics
- Quarterly auto-renewing LP subscriptions
- Average: $350K/quarter within 30 days ($2.8M over 2 years)
- Min LP commitment: $5K-$10K/quarter
- Can publicly market (SEC Rule 506(c))

### Fee Structure
- Management fee: 0-2.5%/year
- Carried interest: 20-30%
- Admin fee: ~0.15%/year

### Strategic Application
- Build public brand alongside investing
- Deploy capital quarterly (no fundraising downtime)
- Most are solo-managed, under $10M AUM
